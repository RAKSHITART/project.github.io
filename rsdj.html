Sustainable Development Goals Mapping

The two case studies, "Efficient Waste Management" and "Store Ranking and Mall Layout", align with several Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) established by the United Nations. Here’s how they map to specific SDGs:

1. Efficient Waste Management System

This system focuses on optimizing waste collection, reducing travel distance, and improving chute placement efficiency.

Relevant SDGs:

  • SDG 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities - Efficient waste management is crucial for creating sustainable urban spaces and reducing the environmental footprint of urban centers like malls.
  • SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production - Emphasizes sustainable waste management practices to minimize waste production and improve recycling.
  • SDG 13: Climate Action - By optimizing waste collection routes, the system reduces fuel consumption and greenhouse gas emissions.
  • SDG 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure - Incorporates innovative algorithms and technology to improve urban infrastructure efficiency.

2. Store Ranking and Mall Layout System

This system optimizes store placement to enhance customer experience and maximize accessibility based on reviews and traffic patterns.

Relevant SDGs:

  • SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth - Boosts economic activity in malls by improving customer accessibility to high-performing stores.
  • SDG 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure - Utilizes advanced algorithms like Dijkstra’s to optimize infrastructure for better performance.
  • SDG 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities - Contributes to well-designed, customer-friendly commercial spaces, enhancing urban quality of life.
  • SDG 17: Partnerships for the Goals - Encourages collaboration between businesses, developers, and urban planners to optimize commercial operations.

By implementing these systems, both projects contribute to the UN’s vision of sustainable development through innovation, improved urban living, and environmentally conscious practices.
