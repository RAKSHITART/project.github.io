Introduction to Project:

Mall management in the context of data structures and algorithms involves efficiently organizing and processing various types of information related to the functioning of a mall. It includes managing stores, inventory, customers, and activities. Data structures and algorithms help optimize tasks such as searching, sorting, data retrieval, and scheduling. Here, I worked on the following topics mentioned below:

1. Optimal Shop Allocation Scenario:

Assign new shop owners to locations based on proximity to anchor stores (like supermarkets or cinema halls) to maximize footfall.

Algorithm:

  • Dijkstra: Finds the shortest distance from anchor stores to various available shop spaces.
  • Bubble Sort: Sorts shop spaces based on their distance to anchor stores.

Explanation:

Represent the mall as a graph with nodes for shops and anchor stores, and edges representing the distance between them. Use Dijkstra to calculate the shortest paths, and Bubble Sort to rank locations based on these distances.

2. Emergency Exit Planning Scenario:

Provide a map for visitors to use the shortest path to exits during emergencies.

Algorithm:

  • Floyd-Warshall: Pre-computes the shortest paths between all points in the mall.
  • BFS: Quickly identifies the best exit path during an emergency.

Explanation:

Model the mall as a graph where locations are nodes and paths are edges. Use Floyd-Warshall to calculate all-pairs shortest paths before any emergency. BFS is used dynamically during an event to direct people to the nearest exits.
